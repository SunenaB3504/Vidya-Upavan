Welcome to Marathi Communication!

In Phase 3, we learn how Marathi sentences are constructed. We focus on the SOV pattern, the three tenses (Past, Present, Future), and phrases for expressing your feelings and needs.

🏗️ How Sentences are Built (SOV Order)

Marathi follows the Subject-Object-Verb order. This means the action word (verb) always comes at the end!

English (SVO) Marathi (SOV) Meaning
I eat a mango मी आंबा खाते। Subject (मी) + Object (आंबा) + Verb (खाते)
Nia speaks Marathi निया मराठी बोलते। Subject (निया) + Object (मराठी) + Verb (बोलते)
We drink water आम्ही पाणी पितो। Subject (आम्ही) + Object (पाणी) + Verb (पितो)

⏳ The Three Tenses (काळ — Kaal)

Verbs change their endings based on gender and time.

Verb Root: जा (jaa) — To Go

Tense I (Boy) I (Girl) We (All)
Present (Now) मी जातो मी जाते आम्ही जातो
Past (Yesterday) मी गेलो मी गेले आम्ही गेलो
Future (Tomorrow) मी जाईन मी जाईन आम्ही जाऊ

❓ Asking Questions & Conversations

Essential Question Words:
  • काय (Kaay) — What
  • कुठे (Kuthe) — Where
  • केव्हा / कधी (Kevha / Kadhi) — When
  • कोण (Kon) — Who
  • कसे (Kase) — How
  • किती (Kiti) — How many / How much

😊 Expressing Opinions & Needs

Marathi uses special constructions for "I want" or "I like".

  • I want: मला ... हवे आहे (Mala ... have aahe)
  • I like: मला ... आवडते (Mala ... avadte)
  • I think: मला वाटते की... (Mala vaatte ki...)
  • I don't want: मला ... नको आहे (Mala ... nako aahe)

🙏 Polite Requests & Commands

Marathi has different levels of respect!

Action To a Friend (Informal) To an Elder (Formal)
Come ये (ye) या (yaa)
Sit बस (bas) बसा (basaa)
Speak बोल (bol) बोला (bolaa)
Go जा (jaa) जा (jaa) / जावे (jaave)
