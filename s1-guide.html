📝 Matras — Vowel Marks

Matras are marks added to consonants to change their vowel sound. The base consonant (with inherent 'a') is shown with each matra.

Matra Vowel Applied to 'क' Sound Example
- a (inherent) क "ka" अक (ak) - eye
ा ā का "kā" काय (kāy) - body
ि i कि "ki" किम् (kim) - what
ी ī की "kī" कीर्ति (kīrti) - fame
ु u कु "ku" कुआर (kuār) - well
ू ū कू "kū" कूप (kūp) - tank
ृ ṛ कृ "kṛ" कृषि (kṛṣi) - farming
े e के "ke" केन (ken) - by whom
ै ai कै "kai" कैतव (kaitav) - deceit
ो o को "ko" कोश (kosh) - treasury
ौ au कौ "kau" कौस्तुभ (kaustubh) - jewel

🔗 Combined Letters & Tricks

Consonants can combine to form clusters. Here are useful patterns and tricks:

Halant (्) — The Virama

What is Halant? The halant (also called virama) is a symbol (्) that removes the inherent vowel from a consonant, making it a pure consonant sound.

Example: क् (k without 'a') + त = क्त (kta)

Common Consonant Clusters

Cluster Words with Cluster Pronunciation Meaning
क्त युक्त, शक्ति "kta" joined, power
ष्ट राष्ट्र, पष्ट "ṣṭa" nation, clear
न्द चन्द्र, बन्द "nda" moon, closed
म्य स्मय, आत्मा "mya" marvel, soul
न्त शान्त, अन्त "nta" peaceful, end

💡 Tips for Reading Combined Letters

  • Left to Right: Read consonant clusters from left to right
  • Identify the Halant: Look for the halant (्) symbol to find where the cluster begins
  • Practice Slowly: Break down clusters into individual sounds first
  • Repeat: Say each cluster multiple times to build muscle memory
  • Common Patterns: क्त, ष्ट, न्द, म्य appear frequently in Sanskrit

⭐ Quick Reference Tips

🎯 Learning Order

  • Start with vowels (अ से औ)
  • Learn consonants group by group (वर्ग)
  • Practice matras with each consonant
  • Then explore combined letters

🏆 Practice Tips

  • Write each letter multiple times
  • Say it aloud to practice pronunciation
  • Look for the letters in simple words
  • Use the examples provided to understand context
